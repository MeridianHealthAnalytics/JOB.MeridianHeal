Company Overview

Meridian Health Analytics is a leading provider of healthcare data solutions supporting payers, providers, and health tech companies across the United States. Based in Denver with a remote-first workforce, Meridian specializes in medical coding compliance, risk adjustment analytics, population health reporting, and healthcare data integration services. Our mission is to empower value-based care through data accuracy, transparency, and actionable insights.

Position Summary

We are seeking a highly analytical and detail-oriented Risk Adjustment & Clinical Coding Analyst to join our Health Data Operations team. This role plays a critical part in ensuring coding accuracy, documentation integrity, and regulatory compliance across a variety of government and commercial health plans. The ideal candidate will bring hands-on experience with ICD-10-CM and CPT/HCPCS coding, risk score optimization, and healthcare claims data analysis.

This position is fully remote and offers flexibility, growth potential, and the opportunity to work with cross-functional teams in an innovative, mission-driven environment.

Key Responsibilities

  • Perform retrospective and prospective reviews of medical records to support accurate coding and risk score capture.
  • Analyze clinical documentation and claims data to identify gaps, errors, or opportunities related to HCC, RxHCC, and other risk models.
  • Support coding initiatives for value-based programs such as Medicare Advantage, Medicaid Managed Care, and ACA plans.
  • Collaborate with QA and clinical teams to ensure coding accuracy and adherence to official guidelines and payer policies.
  • Assist with the development and implementation of documentation improvement strategies and coding audits.
  • Interpret and apply national coding guidelines, payer-specific policies, and regulatory updates (CMS, NCQA, etc.).
  • Contribute to the preparation of regulatory submissions and ensure accuracy in data formats (e.g., 837, 835).
  • Work cross-functionally with informatics, compliance, and EHR teams to support data integration and reporting projects.

Qualifications

  • Bachelor's degree in Health Information Management, Public Health, Nursing, or a related field; equivalent work experience may be considered.
  • Active professional coding certification such as CPC, CRC, or CCS required.
  • Demonstrated experience in medical coding, risk adjustment, and/or clinical data review in payer, provider, or vendor environments.
  • Proficiency in ICD-10-CM and CPT/HCPCS coding systems.
  • Familiarity with HEDIS measures, risk adjustment models (HCC, RxHCC), and healthcare claims lifecycle.
  • Strong analytical and critical thinking skills; attention to detail and accuracy in coding review.
  • Comfortable working with EHR/EMR platforms and health data tools; experience with analytics or audit platforms is a plus.
  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills; ability to collaborate across teams.
